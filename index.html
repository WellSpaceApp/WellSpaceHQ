Why this exists

Built by a student who got tired of the silence.

The idea came from watching my own teacher run a daily Google Form so students could flag how they were doing before class started. It worked well. But it was a generic form with no real structure, easy to forget, and impossible to build on.

Most students would rather say nothing than say "I'm not okay" in front of everyone. WellSpace gives them a way to say it privately, to the teacher they trust, in ten seconds.

Teachers get a simple dashboard showing who might need a check-in that day. Students control everything they share. Nothing is visible to anyone unless the student explicitly sends it.

This is not a school board system or an official records platform. It is a classroom tool, built the way it should have been built from the start.

How it works

Simple for students. Useful for teachers.

WellSpace runs in any browser with no download or installation. Teachers create a class and share a code. Students join with that code and start checking in.

01
Teacher creates a class
Takes two minutes. Set the subject, schedule, and generate an 8-character code to share with students.
02
Students join and check in
Students enter the code, sign up, and log how they're feeling at the start of each period. Ten seconds. They choose what to share.
03
Teacher sees what matters
The teacher dashboard shows who might need support today, based only on what students have chosen to share.
For educators

Does it need school board approval?

Individual teachers can use WellSpace independently in their own classrooms, the same way you would use Google Forms, Notion, or any other tool you choose to adopt on your own initiative. No board approval is needed for personal classroom use.

However, if your school board requires formal approval for third-party tools, or if you are looking to roll it out school-wide or district-wide, you can follow your board's standard process for third-party software and data agreements. WellSpace is transparent about its privacy practices and happy to provide any documentation your board needs.

That said, we understand that data privacy is a real concern in Ontario schools. Here is exactly how WellSpace handles data.

Is it free?
Yes. No subscriptions, no trials, no hidden fees. Free for teachers and students.
Is student data private?
Yes. Students share only what they choose to share. Nothing is visible by default.
Does it need approval?
Not for individual teacher use. For board-wide rollout, follow your board's standard third-party tool approval process.

How your data is stored

Student data is private by default. Nothing is shared with a teacher unless the student explicitly clicks "Share with Teacher."
Passwords are never stored by WellSpace. Firebase Authentication handles all credentials using industry-standard encryption.
Each user has their own private document. No shared blobs. No cross-user access. Firestore security rules are enforced server-side.
No advertising. No tracking pixels. No analytics. We use Google Firebase, EmailJS, and GitHub Pages only.
Students can delete their account instantly. All data is permanently removed with no retention.
Note on Canadian data storage: Firebase currently stores data on US-based Google servers by default. Canadian data residency (Montreal) is available on Firebase's paid tier. WellSpace is currently on the free tier. If your board requires Canadian-only data hosting, this is the current limitation to be aware of. We recommend using WellSpace as an independent classroom tool rather than a board-sanctioned system until this changes.

Get involved

Help build something that matters.

WellSpace is one student building something real. There are parts of this that are too important to do alone. If any of the roles below sound like you, we want to hear from you.

Plans, films, edits, and posts the videos and graphics that get WellSpace in front of people who have never heard of it. You do not need to show your face. Screen recordings, AI voiceover, and text-on-screen are all fine.

Looks at what is working for similar youth-led and wellness projects, brings back real findings, and helps inform decisions about direction. Good fit if you like digging into information and presenting what you find clearly.
